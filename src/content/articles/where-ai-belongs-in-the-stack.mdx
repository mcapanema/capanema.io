The pressure to "add AI" rarely comes with a clear idea of where it should go. So before adopting anything, I find it useful to sort the work into three buckets — automate, assist, and leave alone — because they demand very different bets.

## Automate, assist, or leave alone

**Automate** the tasks that are high-volume, low-stakes, and tolerant of being occasionally wrong. Here, models earn their keep quietly and the cost of a mistake is small.

**Assist** the work that is high-stakes but still benefits from a fast first draft — where a human stays firmly in the loop and owns the outcome. This is where most durable value sits today, and where the design challenge is making the assistance legible enough to trust.

**Leave alone** the decisions where accountability cannot be delegated and the cost of a confident error is severe. Not everything should be automated, and maturity is knowing which.

<Callout variant="warning" title="The failure mode">
The expensive mistake is putting a probabilistic system on a deterministic-stakes decision — automating what you should only have assisted.
</Callout>

## Match the tool to the stakes

None of this is about the model. It is about honestly grading the stakes of each decision and matching the tool to it. Do that well and AI becomes leverage exactly where leverage is safe — and stays out of the places where it isn't.
