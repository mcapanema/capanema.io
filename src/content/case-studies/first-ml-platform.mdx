## The mandate

Machine learning at the company was a collection of heroic notebooks. Models that worked in research died on the way to production, and no two teams shipped the same way twice. The mandate was to build the first real ML platform: a paved road from idea to production that any team could drive.

## What we changed

We built a governed feature store, a model registry, and a serving layer with the same deployment guarantees as the rest of the platform. Crucially, we made the paved road the easy road — the fastest way to ship a model was also the compliant, observable one.

<Pullquote>
A platform succeeds when the right way to do something is also the path of least resistance.
</Pullquote>

We onboarded teams one at a time, co-building their first model on the platform rather than handing them documentation.

## Outcomes

Nine teams onboarded, time-to-ship cut by 42%, and more than thirty models running in production with monitoring and lineage by default.

## What I'd do again

I'd invest in monitoring and lineage from day one rather than treating them as a fast-follow. They turned out to be the features that made teams trust the platform with anything that mattered.
